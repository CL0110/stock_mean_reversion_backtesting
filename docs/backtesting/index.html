Dow Jones Backtesting Engine

A systematic mean‑reversion strategy benchmarked against the DIA ETF

📌 Overview

This project implements a Python-based backtesting engine to evaluate a mean‑reversion strategy on Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA) constituents. It automates data ingestion, portfolio construction, performance calculation, and visualization — producing reproducible, publication‑ready outputs.

The goal is to demonstrate practical skills in financial analysis, systematic strategy design, and data-driven evaluation.

📈 Strategy Performance

The chart below shows the growth of a $100,000 portfolio from 2016–2025. The mean‑reversion strategy significantly outperformed the DIA ETF benchmark over the period.

These results highlight the potential value of systematic rebalancing based on short-term price deviations.

🧠 Methodology

  • Historical DJIA constituent list extracted automatically
  • Daily adjusted close prices downloaded via yfinance
  • Mean‑reversion signal applied across constituents
  • Equal-weighted portfolio with daily rebalancing
  • Benchmark: DIA ETF
  • Performance metrics: CAGR, volatility, Sharpe ratio, drawdown
